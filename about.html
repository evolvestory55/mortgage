Our Mission: Free Financial Clarity for Every Homebuyer

Mortgage & Loans was built on one belief: every homebuyer deserves access to the same financial tools that mortgage brokers and loan officers use — for free, online, without signing up. Every day, millions of people search for answers: "What will my monthly mortgage payment be on a $400,000 home?" "How much house can I afford on a $90,000 salary?" "Should I refinance my 6.5% mortgage?" "How much interest will I pay over 30 years?" Our platform answers all of these questions instantly, accurately, and at no cost.

Every calculator on this site uses the industry-standard PMT amortization formula — the same mathematical model used by banks, credit unions, and mortgage lenders worldwide. We go beyond basic payment estimates: our tools include FHA mortgage insurance premium (MIP), VA funding fees, private mortgage insurance (PMI) based on loan-to-value ratio, property tax estimates, homeowner's insurance, and HOA fees. The result is a true PITI payment — Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance — not a misleading low number that ignores your real monthly obligations.

Our calculator suite covers every stage of the homebuying and refinancing journey. Use our Mortgage Affordability Calculator to find your maximum home price based on income, debts, and down payment. Use the Refinance Calculator to calculate your break-even point and lifetime savings when switching from a 30-year to a 15-year loan, or from a 6.5% to a 5.9% rate. Use the Rent vs Buy Calculator to compare total 10-year cost of renting versus owning. Use the Biweekly Payment Calculator to see how paying every two weeks instead of monthly can shave years off your loan and save tens of thousands in interest. We also support auto loans, personal loans, business/SME loans, and construction loans.

Frequently Asked Questions

01 How much is a monthly mortgage payment on a $300,000 home in 2026?
At a 7.0% interest rate with 20% down ($60,000), a 30-year fixed mortgage on a $300,000 home gives you a loan of $240,000. The principal and interest payment is approximately $1,597/month. Add estimated property tax ($250/month) and homeowner's insurance ($100/month), and you're looking at roughly $1,947/month PITI — before any HOA fees. Use our free Mortgage Calculator to run your exact numbers instantly.
02 Is a 15-year or 30-year mortgage better?
A 15-year mortgage has a higher monthly payment but dramatically lower total interest cost. On a $300,000 loan at 6.5%, a 30-year term costs roughly $382,000 in total interest. The same loan on a 15-year term costs approximately $166,000 in total interest — a savings of over $216,000. The trade-off is a monthly payment about $700 higher. Use our free Loan Comparison Calculator to model both side by side with your actual numbers.
