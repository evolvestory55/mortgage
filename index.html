Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our calculators.

01Are these calculators really free?
Yes — every calculator on our platform is 100% free to use. No sign-up, no subscription, and no hidden fees. Whether you're calculating mortgage payments, auto loan installments, or personal loan costs, you can run unlimited simulations without spending a cent. We believe smart financial planning should be accessible to everyone.
02What formulas do you use?
Our calculators use the industry-standard amortization formula trusted by banks and financial institutions worldwide. Monthly payments are calculated using the PMT formula: M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n − 1], where P = principal, r = monthly interest rate, and n = number of payments. All results are aligned with current market interest rates to ensure accuracy you can actually rely on.
03Is this financial advice?
No. All results generated by our tools are for informational and educational purposes only and do not constitute professional financial advice. Before committing to any loan or financial product, we strongly recommend consulting with a licensed financial advisor or your bank. Our calculators are designed to help you explore scenarios, compare options, and walk into any conversation with your lender fully informed.
04Do you store my data?
Never. Every figure you enter — loan amount, interest rate, income, or repayment term — is processed entirely within your browser and is never sent to or stored on our servers. We don't collect, sell, or share your personal information with any third party. You don't even need to create an account. Your privacy is fully protected every time you use our tools.
05Which loan types do you support?
We currently support the following loan types, with more being added regularly:

🏠 Mortgage / Home Loan — Full amortization schedules, total interest cost, and monthly payment breakdown
🚗 Auto Loan — New and used vehicle financing calculations
💳 Personal Loan — Consumer credit installment planning
🏢 Business / SME Loan — Corporate financing simulations
📊 Loan Comparison Tool — Side-by-side analysis of multiple loan offers

Each calculator is built to give you a clear, accurate picture of your true borrowing cost.
